Net Neutrality
and the Marco Civil bill

What is net neutrality?

  • The principle that Internet service providers (ISP) and governments should treat all data on the Internet equally.
  • Not discriminating or charging differently regardless of:
    • user
    • content
    • site
    • platform
    • application
    • type of attached equipment
    • modes of communication

What are the arguments in favor?

  • Control of data - common carriage agreement
    • Prevent ISPs from deciding which Web sites go fast or slow and which won't load at all
  • Digital rights and freedoms
    • Keep the Internet free and open, fostering democratic communication
  • Allow Competition and Innovation
    • Prevent ISPs from charging websites for high speed page loads
    • Allow a level playing field for all companies and organisations

What are the arguments against?

  • Innovation and Investment
    • Prioritisation of bandwidth may be necessary for future innovation on the Internet
    • ISPs want to provide tiered services to their clients
  • User Welfare
    • Easier to protect users when ISPs have more control over what travels across the network
  • Bandwidth Availability
    • By limiting some types of traffic, ISPs can make more bandwidth available to give more users a better service
